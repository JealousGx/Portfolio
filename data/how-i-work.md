# How I Work

## Who I am

Full-stack software engineer, ~3 years of experience. Based in Pakistan, open to relocation. I work across freelance client projects, my own products, and job hunting in parallel.

## Stack

- **Languages/Frameworks:** Node.js, TypeScript, Go, NestJS, React, Next.js, Vue and more.
- **Cloud/Infra:** AWS (serverless-heavy), GCP, Docker, CI/CD pipelines
- **Databases:** PostgreSQL, DynamoDB, MongoDB
- **Testing:** Jest, Playwright
- **Open source:** Active contributor to Sentry, Twenty CRM, and Motia

Go is a differentiator I don't always lead with, but I use it for performance-sensitive backend work.

## How I approach unclear requirements

I ask clarifying questions before writing any code. I don't guess at architecture or intent — if a requirement is ambiguous, I'll flag it and ask rather than build something that might miss the mark. This saves rework later.

## How I scope work

I balance speed and quality based on how critical the feature is. Core, high-impact features get built carefully and correctly the first time. Lower-stakes or exploratory features get shipped fast so I can iterate based on real feedback. I don't apply one speed to everything — I match the approach to what's actually at stake.

## How I communicate on projects

Frequent updates, even small ones. Clients and teammates shouldn't have to ask "where are we at" — I'd rather over-communicate progress than let something go quiet and cause anxiety on the other end.

## What people say about working with me

Reliable and detail-oriented. I follow through on what I commit to, and I catch the small things (edge cases, error handling, naming, docs) that get skipped under deadline pressure.

## Real project examples

- **Scalere Design (client work):** Reduced load times by 75% using CloudFront, Lambda, and NeonDB.
- **PennyCanny (client work):** Ongoing freelance engagement.
- **Prospkt (personal product):** A lead-finding/scoring tool. Built with TanStack Start, Better Auth, Shadcn, Tailwind v4, Drizzle ORM, TiDB/PostgreSQL, and Gemini Flash for AI features.

## Working principles (simple version)

1. Ask before assuming.
2. Ship the simplest thing that works — don't over-engineer.
3. Don't touch what isn't part of the task.
4. Say "I'm not sure" when I'm not sure, instead of guessing.
